Explanation & Details
Crit Scoring: The total percentage value of rolled crit rate/damage out of the given maximum per echo. Example: Rolling 6.3 (the lowest possible) out of 10.5 (the maximum) crit chance (without any crit damage) would result in a crit scoring of 60 (60% of the possible gain) further increased to 90, due to the 1.5 weight modifier.

This rating is only visible on characters that include crit stats in their weighted stats. Tiers: The segmentation on stat bars displayes the different possible roll values. The higher the tier of the roll, the more segments are highlighted. Possible substats in Wuthering Waves are pre-determined (meaning, for example, you can't roll 6.7% ATK% — only 6.4% or 7.1%) and heavily weighted (the lowest being almost 2.5x more likely to appear compared to the highest).

More information can be found in the "Substat Distribution" table below. Weighted: The total percentage value of all stats weighted by the listed character-specific modifiers. This rating is displayed in a letter-based format for easier understanding. While character-specific weighting is applied, secondary requirements like energy regen and crit ratios are not checked and can result in performing below expectations. Always use this stat with your current crit scoring and character-specific secondary requirements in mind. Echo Value: A rating based on weighted stats further being adjusted by (accummulated) probabilities of the given tiers, (slightly) increasing the value of higher rolled stats instead of using a equal distribution. This gives a even better insight in how much "value" a given echo has and how replaceable it is, with the lowest valued echo being the most replaceable.
